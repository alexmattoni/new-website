Crew Chief Training

The Crew Chief (“CC”) is operationally in charge of the duty crew. They are responsible for supervising the crew and providing proper patient care. A Probationary Crew Chief is one who may act as the Crew Chief if a Crew Chief Trainer is on the crew. A “full” Crew Chief can complete a crew and manages patient care during a call. A Crew Chief Trainer may supervise Probationary Crew Chiefs during calls, and provide training and classes. The following training requirements must be completed:

Probationary Crew Chief

To be a probationary Crew Chief a member must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Be an Attendant
  • Hold valid certification as Emergency Medical Technician – Basic or above in New York
  • Complete the online NIMS IS-100, IS-200, IS-700 and IS-800 courses
  • Complete in-service training for Albuterol, Glucometry, Epinephrine, Patella reduction and Naloxone treatment modalities
  • Take and pass the Crew Chief class and written exam with an 80% or higher
  • Receive instruction in PCR writing from a crew chief trainer and demonstrate proficiency using the ePCR platform.
  • Complete the Crew Chief checklist
  • Teach at least three attendant checklist items under the supervision of a credentialed member
  • Pass a Crew Chief practical, including PCR writing components
  • Perform a first response vehicle equipment check
  • Complete a minimum of two real calls with a Crew Chief Trainer in the patient compartment and receive a passing evaluation for both
  • Recieve joint approval from the captain and training committee

Crew Chief

To be a Crew Chief a member must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Be a Probationary Crew Chief
  • Crew Chief at least two (2) calls, one of which must be a transport, and receive a passing evaluation
  • Lead a training drill, scenario, or other agency training event
  • Recieve joint approval from the captain and training committee

Crew Chief Trainer

To be a Crew Chief Trainer a member must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Complete at least ten (10) shifts as a Crew Chief or have been a CC for at least three (3) months while in service
  • Crew Chief three (3) emergency or non- emergency calls
  • Teach at least three (3) Crew Chief Checklist items to a trainee under the supervision of a Crew Chief Trainer
  • Teach a training scenario or Crew Chief class to the membership under the supervision of a Crew Chief trainer
  • Recieve joint approval from the captain and training committee
