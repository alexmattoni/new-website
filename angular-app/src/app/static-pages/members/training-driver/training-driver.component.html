Driver Training

The Driver is in charge of the safe operation of the Ambulance and the safe transportation of the crew and patient on calls. Attendants may train to become a Driver under the supervision of a qualified Driver Trainer. The following are the minimum promotional requirements for a Driver:

Probationary Driver

To be a probationary Driver a member must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Be an Attendant
  • Hold and actively utilize a valid NYS Class D Drivers License or equivalent for a minimum of six (6) months
  • Attend and pass the written exam for the RPI Ambulance Driver Training Classs
  • Hold a CEVO, EVOC or RPI Ambulance EVDD Certification
  • Complete the Driver Checklist
  • Complete the online NIMS IS-100, IS-200, IS-700 and IS-800 courses
  • Complete and submit copy of an Ambulance Vehicle Checklist
  • Complete at least five (5) hours of behind the wheel training under the supervision of a Driver Trainer
  • Drive the first response vehicle with a driver trainer
  • Drive a minimum of two (2) calls in non-emergency mode and receive passing evaluations from a trainer. A driver trainer must accompany the trainee in the driver compartment during these calls
  • Drive a minimum of one (1) call in emergency mode and receive passing evaluations from a trainer. A driver trainer must accompany the trainee in the driver compartment during this call
  • Drive to each Albany area hospital with a driver trainer
  • Teach three (3) attendant checklist items under the supervision of a credentialed member
  • Pass the Driver Practical Exam administered by a Driver Trainer
  • Recieve joint approval from the captain and training committee

Driver

To be a Driver a member must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Be a Probationary Driver
  • Drive two (2) calls as a probationary Driver and receive passing evaluations from a Trainer
  • Recieve joint approval from the captain and training committee

Driver Trainer

To be a Driver Trainer a member must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Complete at least ten (10) shifts as a Driver or have been a Driver for at least three (3) months while in service
  • Drive three (3) EMS calls, at least one (1) of which is emergent. Emergent call requirement may be waived at the discretion of the Training Committee
  • Teach a driver related course or drill under the supervision of a Driver Trainer
  • Teach at least three (3) Driver Checklist items to a trainee under the supervision of a Driver Trainer
  • Conduct at least one (1) vehicle training session with a trainee under the supervision of a Driver Trainer
  • Recieve joint approval from the captain and training committee
